walk · talk · meditate is open source. Not just the code — the idea itself.

## The Vision

A decentralized, self-sustaining collective for elevating consciousness through continuous, recursive growth. No central authority, no membership fees, no gatekeeping. Just a framework anyone can pick up and use.

## Fork It

Anyone can take this framework and make it their own:

- Run your own pilgrimage with your own group
- Adapt the format to your culture, landscape, and traditions
- Translate it into your language
- Add your own questions, practices, and rituals
- Change what doesn't work. Keep what does.

You don't need permission. You don't need to ask. That's the point.

## Contribute Back

If you discover something that works — a new format, a better question, a practice that deepens the experience — contribute it back to the commons.

The [source lives on GitHub](https://github.com/walktalkmeditate/walktalkmeditate). Open a pull request, start a discussion, or simply share your experience so others can learn from it.

## The Massive Transformative Purpose

By open-sourcing this, we hope people will be inspired to start their own pilgrimages around the world. Each one slightly different, each one authentic to its participants, but all sharing the same core: walk, talk, meditate.

The more groups that form, the more the practice evolves. The more it evolves, the more accessible it becomes. This is how ideas grow — not through control, but through release.
